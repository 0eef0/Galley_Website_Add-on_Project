The History of Lamps

A Crash Course on Lamps

  • Way waaaaaay back 70000 BCE, the first lamp was born. Being that this was still the stone age, it used ver primitive methods. What people would do is that they would hollow out a shell or rock, put some moss and animal fat inside and then ignite.
  • In China and Egypt, the shells and rocks were replaced by pottery and bronze.
  • The Romans made an important development, being the shaping of lamps into animals and other figures.
  • The next major development was when petroleum oil began to be pumped in the 1850's. This led to most lamps converting being petroleum powered
  • As electricity overtook gas as a powersource, many scientists raced to be the one to make and effective electric lamp. This is when our lord and savior, Thomas Edison, created the first iteration of the modern lightbulb, using a carbonized thread and an electrical current to emit light.
